lyman-α forest emulator

The Lyman-α forest is a series of absorption features in the spectrum of quasars (extremely bright galactic cores). As light travels from the quasar towards us, two effects combine to create the observed forest: the expansion of the universe redshifts the spectrum, and these redshifted photons are absorbed and scattered by hydrogen in the intergalactic medium (IGM). Since overdensities will absorb more photons, the Lyman-α forest is effectively a one-dimensional density map along the line of sight.

The figure below is an example of the observed Lyman-α forest, from O'Meara et al. APJ 552 718, arXiv: 0011179.

Because Lyman-α forest observations probe non-linearities (the overdensities along the line of sight), simulations are required to interpret observations. In a series of work I improved simulations of the Lyman-α forest (first paper below), developed a multi-fidelity Gaussian process emulator for the Lyman-α forest (second paper below), and used observations with this emulator to constrain cosmological model parameters (third paper below).
