Martin A. Fernandez

about me

I am postdoctoral researcher in the atmospheric science department at Colorado State University, where I work in the Barnes group. My research uses machine learning methods to improve climate predictions and weather forecasting.

I received my Ph.D in physics from the University of California Riverside where I worked with Simeon Bird. My research involved machine learning and various topics in cosmology, including cosmic strings and the Lyman-α forest.

For my undergraduate education, I studied physics and astronomy at Western Washington University. While there, I had the opportunity to work with Kevin Covey, researching binary stars. I also worked with Brad Johnson and Janelle Leger, researching thin film structures.
